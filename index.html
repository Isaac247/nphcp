Welcome to the Nupe Heritage Cekpa project

About

In a bid to promote and preserve the cultural identity of the Nupe tribe, a project was launched in the Summer, 2021. This project is aimed at taking the Nupe culture round the world, cultural preservation, education, and advocacy. The mastermind behind this project is Dr. Bernice Ede Bodi Malaika Rumala and she is working with a group of brilliant Nupe youths to research and educate about the rich culture of the Nupe land. Being a descendant of the Cekpa village, a Nupe town in Niger state,
Dr. Bernice Ede Bodi Malaika Rumala is committed to preserving the deep and hidden history of the Cekpa people and the Nupe people at large.
The Cekpa people from history are mighty warriors that many dread to face. They fought for the Nupe kingdom. This is outlined in the inaugural song for the Nupe Heritage Cekpa Project called: Cekpa Village Our Home Our place. They are popularly identified with tribal marks on their faces because of the adventures. Some of the history of Cekpa village and migration can be found in this 2020 account provided by Nupe Heritage Cekpa Project elder Dr. Samuel Yisa Zhiri Rumala in an interview conducted by Dr. Bernice Ede Bodi Malaika Rumala: The Nupe Heritage Cekpa Project is a multi-innovative effort that includes a Channel title Nupe People, Places and Things, building of an innovation center for youth through elderly, local and global educational awareness and advocacy of Nupe culture, and humanitarian endeavors including but not limited to supporting entrepreneurship, food distributions, and scholarships.
